Cookie Policy

Last Updated: June 9, 2025

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies and similar storage technologies are, how ToolDuck.xyz uses them, and what choices you have regarding their use. This policy should be read alongside our Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by a web server when you visit a website. They allow the website to recognize your browser on future visits and store certain information. ToolDuck.xyz uses a combination of browser localStorage (which persists data locally without expiry unless cleared) and third-party advertising cookies.

2. Our First-Party Local Storage Usage

ToolDuck.xyz uses your browser's localStorage API — not traditional HTTP cookies — to store a minimal set of user preference values. These are stored exclusively on your device and are never transmitted to our servers or any third party.

Storage Key Purpose Type Expiry
toolduck-theme Remembers your color scheme preference Functional / Preference Persistent (until cleared)
toolduck-cookie-consent Records whether you have accepted or declined the cookie notice Functional / Consent Persistent (until cleared)

These local storage entries contain no personally identifiable information and are classified as strictly necessary for basic functionality (theme preference) and consent management. They do not require explicit consent under most regulatory frameworks, but we disclose them here in the spirit of full transparency.

3. Third-Party Advertising Cookies — Google AdSense

ToolDuck.xyz serves advertisements through the Google AdSense network. Google and its advertising technology partners may place cookies on your device when you visit pages on our Platform. These cookies are used to:

  • Serve advertisements that are relevant to you based on your browsing history on our site and other sites across the internet
  • Measure the performance and effectiveness of advertising campaigns
  • Prevent the same ad from being shown to you repeatedly
  • Detect and prevent fraudulent ad impressions

Key Google Advertising Cookies

__gads Google Google AdSense advertising cookie; tracks ad engagement 13 months

4. Third-Party Analytics Cookies

We may use Google Analytics with IP anonymization enabled to understand aggregate patterns of how visitors use our tools — for example, which tools are accessed most frequently or which browsers are most common. This data is anonymized and aggregated; it cannot be used to identify individual users. You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking across all websites by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

5. How to Manage and Delete Cookies

You have several options for controlling cookies:

  • Browser Settings: All modern browsers allow you to block, delete, or manage cookies through their privacy or settings menus. Note that blocking certain cookies may impair the functionality of our advertising partners' systems but will not affect the functionality of our tools themselves.
  • Google Ads Settings: Visit google.com/settings/ads to manage personalized ad preferences across Google's advertising network.
  • NAI Opt-Out: Visit the Network Advertising Initiative opt-out page to opt out of interest-based advertising from multiple providers at once.
  • DAA Opt-Out: Visit aboutads.info for the Digital Advertising Alliance opt-out tool.
  • Clear Local Storage: You can clear ToolDuck.xyz local storage entries at any time via your browser's Developer Tools (Application → Local Storage) or by clearing site data in your browser's privacy settings.

6. Do Not Track (DNT)

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal. Currently, there is no industry-wide standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals. We monitor the development of a DNT standard and will update this policy accordingly when a consensus emerges. Our first-party local storage usage is not affected by DNT signals as it is entirely preference-based.

7. Changes to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, regulation, or our use of third-party services. When we do, we will update the "Last Updated" date above. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

8. Contact

If you have any questions about our use of cookies or local storage, please visit our Contact page.
